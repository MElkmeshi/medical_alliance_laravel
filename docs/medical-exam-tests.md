## 10. Psychological Assessments (Physician-scored)

### Kessler Psychological Distress Scale (K10)

10 questions rated 1–5 (None of the time → All of the time) covering the past 4 weeks:

1. Tired out for no good reason
2. Feeling nervous
3. So nervous nothing could calm you down
4. Feeling hopeless
5. Restless or fidgety
6. So restless you could not sit still
7. Feeling depressed
8. Everything was an effort
9. So sad nothing could cheer you up
10. Feeling worthless

**Output:** Total score (10–50)

### Epworth Sleepiness Scale

8 situations rated 0–3 (No chance → High chance of dozing):

1. Sitting and reading
2. Watching TV
3. Sitting inactive in a public place
4. As a passenger in a car for an hour
5. Lying down to rest in the afternoon
6. Sitting and talking to someone
7. Sitting quietly after lunch without alcohol
8. In a car, stopped for a few minutes in traffic

**Output:** Total score (0–24)

## 11. Family & Medical History (Employee self-report)

### 48-Item Yes/No Medical History

Key categories:
- **ENT & Respiratory:** Sinus trouble, ear discharge, asthma/bronchitis, hay fever, shortness of breath, coughed blood, tuberculosis
- **Cardiovascular:** Heart disease, rheumatic fever, abnormal heartbeat, high blood pressure, stroke, serious chest pain
- **Digestive:** Abdominal pain, stomach ulcer, recurrent indigestion, jaundice/hepatitis, gall bladder disease, change in bowel habits, blood in stool
- **Neurological/Mental:** Headaches/migraine, dizziness/fainting, epilepsy, mental condition treatment, drug/alcohol abuse
- **Musculoskeletal:** Joints/spinal trouble, accidents/fractures, varicose veins
- **Metabolic:** Diabetes, change in weight, blood disease, kidney disease, blood in urine
- **Other:** Cancer, skin trouble, tropical disease, fear of heights, surgical operations
- **Occupational exposure:** Mercury, radioactivity, toxic chemicals, excessive noise
- **Women only:** Abnormal PAP smear, gynecological treatment, pregnancy

## 12. Fitness Certificate Outcome

### SLB Certificate (OGUK guidelines)

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Fit without restrictions | Cleared for all oil & gas work |
| Fit with restrictions | One or more R-codes apply (see below) |
| Unfit | Not cleared for oil & gas work |
| Must be reassessed | Requires additional tests or specialist opinion |

### Restriction Codes (R1–R15)

| Code | Restriction |
|---|---|
| R1 | Unfit for offshore, vessels and remote locations |
| R2 | Unfit for lifting and strenuous efforts |
| R3 | Unfit to work in certain countries |
| R4 | Unfit for jobs requiring precise color vision |
| R5 | Unfit to work in areas with high noise levels |
| R6 | Unfit to work in high-risk malaria countries |
| R7 | Unfit to work in extreme heat |
| R8 | Unfit to work in extreme cold |
| R9 | Specific restriction — contact country doctor / international health coordinator |
| R10 | Totally unfit for a temporary period until further notice |
| R11 | Unfit for jobs requiring good visual acuity (e.g. driving company vehicle) |
| R12 | Fit only for a defined period (1, 3, or 6 months) — must be reassessed |
| R13 | Unfit to drive a company vehicle |
| R14 | Unfit to fly long-haul flights |
| R15 | Unfit to work at heights or in confined spaces |
